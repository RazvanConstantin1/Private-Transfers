## About Iași

Iași is the largest city in Romania's northeastern region, with a student population that keeps it perpetually young despite its age. The old city sits on seven hills, each with its own character, and the main boulevard (Ștefan cel Mare) connects landmarks spanning seven centuries.

**Top attractions:**

- **Palace of Culture (Palatul Culturii)** — The defining landmark of Iași. A neo-Gothic palace built 1906–1925 on the site of the former Moldavian princely court. Houses four museums including the National Museum of History of Moldova and a remarkable clock museum.
- **Metropolitan Cathedral (Catedrala Mitropolitană)** — The largest Orthodox cathedral in Romania, consecrated in 1887. The relics of Saint Parascheva, patron saint of Moldova, are housed here and draw hundreds of thousands of pilgrims each October.
- **Three Hierarchs Monastery** — A 17th-century masterpiece of Moldavian architecture, its exterior completely covered in intricate carved stone in lace-like patterns. One of Romania's most ornate buildings.
- **Copou Park and Eminescu's Linden Tree** — The city's oldest park, containing a linden tree where Romania's national poet Mihai Eminescu is said to have written beneath its branches. A literary pilgrimage site.
- **Botanical Garden** — The largest in Romania, sprawling over 100 hectares on the northern edge of the city.
- **Moldavian wine country** — The region surrounding Iași produces some of Romania's best wines. Cotnari (60 km north) is famous for its aromatic dessert wines; Iași wineries offer tastings and tours.

**Is it possible to do a Bucharest–Iași round trip in one day?**  
Possible but demanding. An early departure (05:00–06:00) would give you 5–6 hours in Iași before the return journey. We recommend staying overnight and returning the next day for a relaxed experience.

**Can you continue to the Republic of Moldova?**  
We currently operate within Romanian territory only. Iași is 25 km from the Moldovan border; onward travel is straightforward from there.

**Are there good restaurants in Iași?**  
The city has a thriving food scene. The area around Piața Unirii and the streets between the three main monasteries has good traditional Moldavian and international options.

**What's the road like in winter?**  
The main routes to Iași are well-maintained year-round. Winter driving in the Moldavian hills can involve snow; your driver monitors conditions and will advise if any changes to the route are needed.
