## About Brașov

Brașov is one of Romania's most visited cities for good reason. Founded by Teutonic Knights in the 13th century, the old town is centred around Piața Sfatului (Council Square) — a beautifully preserved medieval square flanked by the Black Church, the oldest and largest Gothic church in Romania.

The city sits at 600 metres altitude in a natural bowl between the Carpathian mountains, which makes it a perfect base for:

- **Hiking** on Tâmpa Mountain (cable car access, or trails through forest)
- **Visiting Bran Castle**, 30 km southwest — the so-called "Dracula's Castle," actually a medieval fortress with a fascinating royal history
- **Skiing at Poiana Brașov**, Romania's premier ski resort, 12 km from the city centre
- **Day trips** to Sinaia Monastery, Peleș Castle, or the Bucegi Plateau

In summer, Brașov's parks and terraces fill with visitors from across Europe. In winter, it transforms into one of Romania's most atmospheric destinations, with Christmas markets and snow-capped peaks.
