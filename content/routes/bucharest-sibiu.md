## About Sibiu

Named a European Capital of Culture in 2007 alongside Luxembourg, Sibiu (German: Hermannstadt) has never lost the cosmopolitan energy that title brought. Today it's one of Romania's most prosperous and beautifully maintained cities.

**Top attractions:**

- **Piața Mare (Large Square)** — The largest of Sibiu's three connected squares, bordered by baroque palaces. The History Museum and BRUKENTHAL National Museum (one of Central Europe's oldest, founded 1817) overlook it.
- **Brukenthal Museum** — An exceptional collection of European painting housed in a baroque palace. The oldest public museum in Romania and Eastern Europe.
- **The Bridge of Lies (Podul Minciunilor)** — A cast-iron bridge from 1859, the first in Romania, with accompanying folklore about liars making it creak.
- **Sibiu Philharmonic** — One of Romania's finest orchestras, performing in a beautiful concert hall in Piața Mare.
- **ASTRA Museum (Muzeul ASTRA)** — The largest open-air ethnographic museum in Romania, 4 km from the centre. Over 300 traditional buildings from across the country, spread across a forested park.
- **Transfăgărășan Road** — 60 km south of Sibiu, the legendary mountain road (DN7C) offers some of the most spectacular driving scenery in Europe. Open June–October. Your driver can extend the journey to include a section.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can we stop at Cozia Monastery on the way?**  
Yes. Cozia is a natural midpoint and the monastery takes about 30 minutes to explore. Please request this when booking.

**Is Sibiu a good base for the Transfăgărășan?**  
Ideal. The southern access to the Transfăgărășan (near Bâlea Lac) is 60 km from Sibiu. If you're staying multiple days, we can arrange a separate excursion.

**What's the best time of year to visit?**  
Sibiu is exceptional in summer (July–August festivals) and December (one of Romania's best Christmas markets). Spring and autumn offer pleasant weather with fewer crowds.

**How far is Sibiu from Brașov?**  
About 145 km (1h 45min). Many visitors do a Bucharest → Sibiu → Brașov → Bucharest loop over three or four days — we can handle each leg.
