## About Sinaia

Called the "Pearl of the Carpathians," Sinaia sits at 860 metres altitude in a narrow valley between forested slopes. King Carol I chose it as his royal summer retreat in 1875, commissioning the extraordinary Peleș Castle — a German Renaissance masterpiece considered one of Europe's most beautiful palaces.

**Top attractions:**

- **Peleș Castle** — A must-see. The original castle (1883) and the smaller Pelișor (1902) stand in immaculate grounds. Guided tours run daily.
- **Sinaia Monastery** — Founded in 1695, the monastery that gave the town its name. Beautiful frescoes and a peaceful courtyard.
- **Sinaia ski slopes** — Three main ski areas served by gondola and chairlift, suitable for all levels. Best season: December to March.
- **Bucegi Plateau** — Cable car from town centre to 2,000 metres, with trails leading to the famous Sphinx and Babele rock formations.
- **Casino Sinaia** — A stunning Art Nouveau building (1913) in the town's central park, hosting cultural events year-round.
