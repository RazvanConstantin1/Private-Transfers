At 460 kilometres, the Bucharest–Cluj-Napoca run is VOLTLANE's most ambitious intercity route — a five-hour drive across the full width of Romania. You cross the Carpathians twice, pass through the heart of Transylvania, and arrive in Romania's unofficial cultural capital. All three of our electric vehicles have the range to complete the journey without stopping to charge.

## What to Expect on a Long-Distance Transfer

For journeys of this length, VOLTLANE builds in a comfort stop — typically 15 minutes at a service area around the halfway point. Your driver will confirm the plan before departure. WiFi is available on the Tesla and Capri. Water is provided on all vehicles.

The route through the Southern Carpathians via Sibiu is particularly scenic — the Olt Valley gorge between Brezoi and Câineni is one of Romania's most dramatic landscapes. Many passengers prefer to sit back and enjoy the views rather than work.

## About Cluj-Napoca

Cluj-Napoca is Romania's second city by some measures and its first by others — it has the country's largest student population, its most vibrant arts scene, and its most internationally recognised music festival (UNTOLD, held every August in the Central Park). The city straddles the Someș River in the Transylvanian plateau, surrounded by gentle hills and vineyard-covered slopes.

**Top attractions:**

- **St. Michael's Church** — A Gothic masterpiece in Unirii Square (Piața Unirii), the largest Gothic church in Romania, dating from the 14th century.
- **National Museum of Transylvanian History** — Housed in a 19th-century building, covering the full arc of Transylvanian history from pre-Roman Dacians to the 20th century.
- **Botanical Garden** — One of Romania's finest, with Japanese, Roman, and Italian sections. Perfect for an afternoon walk.
- **Cetățuie Hill** — A short climb above the old town rewards with panoramic views across the city.
- **Turda Salt Mine (Salina Turda)** — 30 km east of Cluj, this converted salt mine is one of Romania's most remarkable attractions. An underground lake with rowing boats, a Ferris wheel, and ancient salt galleries.
- **UNTOLD Festival** — If your visit falls in late July or early August, Romania's largest music festival transforms the Central Park into a world-class event.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can you do Bucharest–Cluj same day?**  
Yes. Plan for an early morning departure (we recommend before 07:00 to avoid both peak-hour surcharge and motorway traffic). Allow at least 5.5 hours for a relaxed journey with one comfort stop.

**Is the Transylvania motorway (A3) open the full way?**  
The A3 is under continuous construction. Your driver will check the current state before departure and choose the fastest route. Progress varies by season.

**What about one-way vs round-trip?**  
A one-way transfer means your driver returns empty to Bucharest; the price reflects this. A round-trip booking (you need the driver to bring you back on a separate date) saves 10% vs two separate one-ways.

**Do you also do Bucharest–Cluj by return rail transfer?**  
No — we provide the road transfer. The intercity rail connection is excellent if you prefer the train; we can drop you at Gara de Nord.
