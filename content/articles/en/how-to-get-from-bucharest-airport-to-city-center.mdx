You've just landed at Henri Coandă International Airport (OTP) — Bucharest's main international airport, usually called Otopeni after the town it sits in. You're 17 kilometres north of the city center. Now what?

The answer depends on your priorities: budget, speed, comfort, luggage volume, and how much you know about Bucharest already. Here's an honest breakdown of every option, with real prices as of 2026.

## At a Glance — All Options Compared

| Option | Journey time | Price (per person) | Door-to-door? |
|--------|-------------|-------------------|---------------|
| Henri Coandă Express Train | 40 min to Gara de Nord | €1.30 | No — stops at Gara de Nord |

## Option 1: The Henri Coandă Express Train

The airport has its own train connection — the Henri Coandă Express, operated by CFR Călători. It runs between the airport and Gara de Nord (Bucharest's main railway station) in approximately 40 minutes, with departures roughly every 40 minutes.

**Ticket price:** Around 8 RON (€1.30). One of the best bargains in Romanian transport.

**Where to board:** Follow the "Train" signs inside the terminal, down to the basement level. The station is well-marked.

**The catch:** The train terminates at Gara de Nord, not in the city center. Gara de Nord is in the northern part of Bucharest, and while it's on the metro line (M1), getting from there to most hotels and Airbnbs requires either a metro journey (another €0.50-1 but with connections and luggage management on stairs) or a taxi from outside the station (€5-10 depending on destination).

**Who it's best for:** Budget solo travellers staying near Gara de Nord or willing to navigate the metro with a backpack. Not ideal for families with luggage or first-time visitors who aren't familiar with Bucharest's transport geography.

**Practical tips:** 
- Buy tickets at the vending machine in the airport station
- The train is safe, reasonably modern, and air-conditioned
- Check the timetable on CFR's website — trains run until late but not all night
- Your flight arrives after midnight? The train stops running around 22:30-23:00

## Option 2: The 783 Express Bus

Route 783 runs between the airport and Piața Unirii (Union Square) in the city center, roughly every 20-30 minutes during the day and every 40 minutes in the evening. The journey takes 60-90 minutes depending on traffic — significantly longer than the train.

**Ticket price:** Around 2.50 RON (€0.50) from the STB vending machine at the bus stop, or a RATB card.

**Where to board:** Exit the arrivals terminal, turn right, and follow the signs to the bus stops. The 783 stop is clearly marked.

**The route:** The bus makes multiple stops across the northern part of Bucharest before reaching the center. This makes it slower than other options but also more useful if you're staying somewhere along the route (Aviatorilor, Piața Victoriei, etc.).

**The catch:** With luggage, a crowded bus during rush hour is genuinely uncomfortable. The journey time is variable and can extend to 90+ minutes in heavy traffic.

**Who it's best for:** Budget travellers staying near one of the route's stops, or very patient ones going to Piața Unirii.

## Option 3: Official Taxis at the Airport

Bucharest airport taxis have had a complicated reputation, but the situation has improved significantly. The key word is **official**: you want a taxi from the official taxi rank, booked through the automatic taxi machines inside the terminal.

**Machines location:** Inside the arrivals hall, before you exit. Look for the yellow automatic taxi booking machines operated by RATB or individual companies (Pelicantaxi, Speed Taxi, Cobalcescu). These print a receipt with the taxi's number plate and expected price.

**Price:** €15-25 to most city center destinations, depending on the specific location and traffic. The meter runs from airport.

**The unofficial taxi situation:** Unofficial (unmarked) taxi drivers operate in the arrivals area and approach passengers directly. These are not regulated and overcharging is well-documented. If someone approaches you before you reach the official machines, decline.

**Journey time:** 30-45 minutes in normal traffic; up to 75 minutes in rush hour.

**Who it's best for:** Travellers who want door-to-door service at a reasonable price and are comfortable navigating the arrivals hall to the machine.

**Practical tips:**
- Always use the machine inside, never accept unsolicited rides
- The meter should start at the official airport rate (slightly higher than in-city)
- Pelicantaxi and Cobalcescu have consistently good reputations

## Option 4: Uber and Bolt

Uber and Bolt operate at Bucharest airport, and both work well if you know where the pickup area is.

**Price:** €12-20 depending on destination and demand. Surge pricing applies during peak times.

**The important detail:** Uber and Bolt pickups are **not at the terminal exit**. You need to walk about 5 minutes from the arrivals hall to a designated pickup area in the car park. This is manageable but can be confusing with luggage and jet lag.

**Journey time:** 30-45 minutes, same as regular taxis.

**Potential issues:**
- Surge pricing during peak periods (arriving at 17:00 on a Friday will cost more)
- Some drivers decline long-distance trips from the app
- No English guarantee (though most Bucharest drivers know basic phrases)
- No luggage specification — you might get a compact car for large luggage

**Who it's best for:** Regular Uber/Bolt users who know the apps well and are travelling light.

<Callout type="tip">
If using Uber or Bolt, screenshot your driver's plate number and share your ETA with someone before getting in. This is good practice anywhere.
</Callout>

## Option 5: Pre-Booked Private Transfer

This is what VOLTLANE does, and it's worth explaining objectively. A pre-booked private transfer means you confirm your booking, flight number, and destination in advance, and a driver meets you at the arrivals exit with a sign bearing your name.

**Price:** €48-69 depending on vehicle choice (Hyundai Kona Electric, Tesla Model 3, or Ford Capri SUV).

**What's included:**
- Flight tracking — if your flight is delayed or early, the driver adjusts automatically
- Meet & greet in the arrivals hall — no searching for the car
- Fixed price — no surge, no meter running in traffic
- English-speaking driver
- Door-to-door to any Bucharest address
- Luggage assistance

**Journey time:** 30-45 minutes, same as other car options.

**For groups of 3-4 people**, this is often the most cost-effective option when compared to three or four separate Uber rides or two taxis.

**The strongest use cases:**
- Late-night arrivals when taxi queues are long and Uber is surging
- First visit to Bucharest (no stress about navigation)
- Travelling with children, elderly passengers, or significant luggage
- Business travellers who need reliability and English communication
- Continuing directly to another Romanian city (Brașov, Sinaia, Sibiu, etc.)

## Which Option Should You Choose?

The honest answer depends on three factors: group size, luggage, and your comfort with navigating an unfamiliar city.

**Solo traveller, light luggage, low budget:** Take the train (€1.30) to Gara de Nord, then metro or taxi to your destination. The extra 20-30 minutes of navigation is worth the savings.

**Couple or family with luggage:** Private transfer or official taxi. The convenience-to-cost ratio is strong once you factor in two people's time, luggage stress, and the inconsistency of metered taxi pricing.

**Group of 3-4 people:** Pre-booked private transfer is competitive on per-person cost and offers the most comfort.

**Late-night arrival:** Private transfer. Taxi queues at 2am are real, Uber surge pricing spikes, and the train has stopped running.

**Going directly to another city:** Private transfer is the only realistic door-to-door option. For Brașov, Sinaia, or Sibiu direct from the airport, a private transfer saves hours.

**Business traveller:** Pre-booked private transfer. The reliability, English communication, and fixed cost align with business travel needs.

## Practical Tips for Arriving at Bucharest Airport

- **Arrivals** are on the ground floor of the terminal. Follow the crowd from the aircraft.
- **Baggage claim** can be slow — Romanian flights sometimes take 30-45 minutes for bags to appear. Be patient.
- **Currency:** RON (Romanian Leu) is the currency. ATMs are in the arrivals hall at decent rates. You won't need cash for private transfers (card/online payment) but it's useful for tips.
- **SIM cards:** Available in the terminal from Orange, Vodafone, and Digi — useful for navigation.
- **Tourist information:** The Visit Romania desk in arrivals can answer general questions.
- **Smoking:** Prohibited inside the terminal; designated areas are outside.
- **Baggage storage:** Available in the terminal for layovers.

For most international visitors, the choice comes down to whether you value speed and convenience over price. The train is excellent value if navigating public transport is something you're comfortable with. For everyone else, a private transfer removes every uncertainty from the first 45 minutes in Romania.
