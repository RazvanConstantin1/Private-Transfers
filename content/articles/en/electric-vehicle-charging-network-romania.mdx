Romania's public EV charging infrastructure has grown dramatically since 2022. What was once a sparse and unreliable network has become something genuinely usable for cross-country travel — not yet on par with Germany or the Netherlands, but serviceable for most itineraries with sensible planning.

This guide covers what you actually need to know for an EV road trip in Romania in 2026: the main networks, where to charge, which apps work, and what to expect.

## The Current State of EV Infrastructure in Romania

Romania has approximately 2,800 public charging points as of early 2026 — up from around 700 in 2022, representing 400% growth in four years. The distribution is uneven: Bucharest is well-served, the major highway corridors have workable coverage, and rural areas are still sparse.

Key facts:
- DC fast charging (CCS/CHAdeMO) is available at major fuel stations and highway rest stops
- AC charging (Type 2) is more widely distributed, including at hotels, supermarkets, and city parking
- Tesla Superchargers are available at 12+ locations nationally
- Coverage is significantly better on the Bucharest-Brașov, Bucharest-Constanța, and Bucharest-Cluj corridors than on secondary routes

## The Major Charging Networks

### Renovatio (Largest Network)

Renovatio is the largest public charging network in Romania, operating over 500 charge points across the country. They operate a mix of AC 22kW stations and DC fast chargers up to 150kW at highway locations.

**Coverage:** Strong in Bucharest (multiple locations in commercial centers, hotels, and parking garages) and along main corridors. Patchy in rural areas.

**Pricing:** Approximately €0.35-0.45/kWh for DC fast charging; €0.20-0.30/kWh for AC.

**App:** Renovatio app (iOS/Android). RFID card also available.

**Reliability:** Generally good at newer stations; older urban stations have higher downtime rates.

### Enel X Way

Enel X Way (formerly Enel X) operates a growing network focused on urban centres and partnership locations (shopping malls, offices). AC-dominant with some DC.

**Coverage:** Good in Bucharest, Cluj-Napoca, Timișoara, Iași.

**Pricing:** Similar to Renovatio; pricing via the Enel X Way app.

**Best for:** Destination charging during shopping or dining.

### MOL Plugee

MOL's EV charging network, co-located with MOL fuel stations along major roads. Particularly useful for highway charging.

**Coverage:** Major highways (A1, A2, DN1) and primary national roads.

**Pricing:** Around €0.40-0.50/kWh for DC.

**Advantage:** Open 24/7, well-lit, safe locations at existing fuel stations.

### E.On Drive

E.On's charging network is growing rapidly in Moldavia and northern Romania, areas where other networks are thinner.

**Coverage:** Good in Iași, Bacău, Suceava region.

### PetrOM Electrify (OMV Petrom)

Romania's largest fuel retailer has been adding EV chargers to its network. DC fast chargers at strategic locations, useful for highway travel.

**Coverage:** Multiple locations on A1, A2, A3 motorways and DN1.

Tesla operates Supercharger V3 stations (up to 250kW) at multiple locations in Romania. As of 2026:

**Active Supercharger locations:**

- Sibiu (1 station)
- Cluj-Napoca (1 station)

## The Best Apps for EV Travel in Romania

### PlugShare (Essential)

PlugShare aggregates charger data from multiple networks and user reports. It's the most comprehensive map of all charger types in Romania, including slow AC home chargers shared by owners.

**What it does well:** Shows real-time availability reports (user check-ins), charging speed, and connector types. User community is active in Romania.

**Use it for:** Finding chargers off the main corridors, checking if a specific station is working, and locating slower AC chargers at hotels.

### ABRP (A Better Routeplanner)

ABRP is the best route planning tool for non-Tesla EVs. Enter your starting point, destination, and vehicle model; it calculates the optimal charging stops based on your battery and the available networks.

**Romanian coverage:** Good. The tool has accurate data on Romanian chargers.

**Use it for:** Planning a multi-stop road trip (e.g., Bucharest → Brașov → Sibiu → Cluj) with specific charging recommendations.

### Network-Specific Apps

For payment and activation, you'll typically need the network operator's app:
- **Renovatio app** — for Renovatio stations (most common)
- **Enel X Way app** — for Enel stations
- **MOL Pay** — for MOL Plugee stations
- **Tesla app** — for Superchargers

Carry the apps pre-installed and pre-registered before your trip. Some networks require account creation before you can activate a charger.

## EV Road Trip Planning: Bucharest → Brașov → Sibiu → Cluj

Here's a practical example of a multi-city Transylvania itinerary with charging:

**Bucharest to Brașov (166 km)**
- No charging needed if starting with >50% charge — range is well within typical EV range
- If you want to top up: Renovatio stations in Ploiești (100 km in) or Tesla Supercharger in Ploiești

**Brașov to Sibiu (148 km)**
- Direct: most modern EVs can handle this without intermediate charging
- Optional charge in Brașov before departure: Tesla Supercharger in Brașov

**Sibiu to Cluj-Napoca (148 km)**
- Charge in Sibiu: Tesla Supercharger or Renovatio DC
- The Transylvania plateau section has limited coverage, so start this leg with adequate charge

**Total: 460 km across three days, 2-3 charging sessions needed (20-40 min each)**

## Common Challenges to Expect

**Broken chargers:** DC fast chargers in Romania have a higher downtime rate than in Western Europe — typically 10-15% of stations have some kind of fault at any given time. Always have a backup plan (secondary charger location within range).

**Payment methods:** Some networks require a pre-registered RFID card (no walk-up payment). Verify payment options before you need them. Renovatio and MOL accept card payments at the station or via app.

**Occupied stalls:** Popular DC chargers (especially Superchargers in Brașov and Cluj) can be full during summer weekends. ABRP shows occupancy for Tesla Superchargers; for others, check PlugShare.

**Speed variation:** A charger rated at 150kW may deliver only 50-80kW depending on your vehicle's acceptance rate, battery temperature, and state of charge. Plan accordingly.

**Rural coverage gaps:** If your itinerary includes rural Moldavia, Maramureș, or Dobrogea, plan carefully. Coverage exists but is thinner, and distances between chargers may push range limits for smaller batteries.
